“Smart food” Strives to Redefine Gastronomy in France

But powdered food and energy bars are still having a tough time altering French people’s deep-rooted preference for elaborate and savory cuisine

22 November, 2018 Yingke Zhao &
Luis Miguel Cabrera

Meal replacement, usually marketed as “smart food”, is now growingly popular in France, although powdered food and energy bars are still having a tough time altering French people’s deep-rooted preference for elaborate and savory cuisine. “It looks like you are becoming astronautes”, a Parisian lady jokes with two young men buying bottled nutrient powder for lunch, “but maybe one day I will try it, too.”
